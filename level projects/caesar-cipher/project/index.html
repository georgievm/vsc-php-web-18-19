CaesarCipher

Caesar cipher is one of the simplest and most widely known encryption techniques. It is a type of substitution cipher in which each letter in the plaintext is replaced by a letter some fixed number (shift) of positions down or up the alphabet, as shown.

The "shift" number can be:
positive - the letters for shifting begin at the beginning of the alphabet
negative- the letters begin at the alphabet end (as on the image above)

The purpose of my web-app is to decoding and for it I use automaticly calculated shift:
shift = [each alph letter pos] - [most frequently text symbol pos]
*the alphabet(alph) is used to determine the positions(pos)

The most frequent letters in English are e, h and t.

That's precisely why firstly there will be shown the variants in which these 3 letters involved in the shift formula. By pressing the "Show all" button there will be shown all variants.
